AI-Bio Research, Strategy, Product Development, and Scientific Communication

We help biotech, bioinformatics, AI, and biosecurity organizations understand, evaluate, build, and communicate work at the frontier of AI and biology.

Burns LSC works with teams facing ambiguous AI-Bio questions: what is real, what is useful, what is risky, what should be built next, and how should it be explained to the people who need to act on it?

The AI-Bio frontier

AI is making routine bioinformatics easier to automate. The more valuable questions now sit upstream:

  • Which AI capabilities actually work in biological contexts?
  • Which workflows can be automated, augmented, or turned into products?
  • Which demos are real, and which are brittle or misleading?
  • Where do frontier AI systems create new biological risks?
  • What should an organization build, buy, evaluate, or avoid?
  • How should complex AI-Bio work be communicated to users, funders, customers, or leadership?

These questions require biological judgment, computational depth, product sense, clear communication, and disciplined execution.

That is where we work.

What we do

We help organizations make better decisions and build useful systems at the intersection of AI and biology.

Our work combines:

  • Computational biology
  • Single-cell and spatial omics
  • High-dimensional cytometry
  • AI-assisted scientific workflows
  • LLM and agent evaluation
  • Bioinformatics product strategy
  • AI-biosecurity research
  • Prototype and product development
  • Science communication
  • Project operations

The goal is not to “add AI.” The goal is to find where AI creates real leverage, where it is likely to fail, what should be built, and how the work should be translated into action.
